## **Project Idea: Living Paris**

We are building **Living Paris**, an AI-powered city companion that helps people explore Paris through a conversation instead of traditional map searches.

Unlike Google Maps, users don't search for places like "restaurants near me." Instead, they describe what they want to do, such as:

- "Plan a romantic evening under €50."
- "Show me quiet cafés within a 10-minute walk."
- "I only have 3 hours before my train."
- "It's raining now."
- "I'm traveling with kids."

The AI understands the user's intent and transforms the map in real time. It highlights the best places, suggests walking routes, changes map layers, and recommends personalized experiences using Paris Open Data.

The application combines AI, interactive maps, animations, and public datasets to make the city feel alive. As the conversation continues, the recommendations and map update dynamically based on new preferences.

### **Core Features**

- 💬 Natural language and voice conversations
- 🗺️ Interactive Mapbox map with animations
- ✨ Dynamic map updates based on user intent
- 🚶 Smart walking route generation
- 📊 Paris Open Data integration (cafés, parks, metro, noise, air quality, accessibility, etc.)
- 🎯 Personalized experiences like Date Night, Hidden Gems, Family Day, Photography Tour, and Rainy Day
- 🎨 Smooth UI animations and cinematic map transitions

### **Tech Stack**

- **Frontend:** Next.js, React, Tailwind CSS
- **Maps:** Mapbox GL JS, react-map-gl
- **Animations:** Framer Motion, [Deck.gl](http://Deck.gl)
- **Spatial Processing:** Turf.js
- **AI:** Gemini / OpenAI / Claude
- **Backend:** Next.js API Routes
- **Data:** Paris Open Data (GeoJSON)
- **Deployment:** Vercel

### **Goal**

The goal is not to build another chatbot or map application. We want to create an AI-first city experience where users feel like they are **having a conversation with Paris**, and the city responds by reshaping itself to match their needs in a beautiful, interactive, and memorable way. This creates a demo that is visually impressive, intuitive, and perfectly suited for a hackathon focused on user experience and innovation.
